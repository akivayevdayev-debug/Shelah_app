Accessibility Statement

Last updated: June 2026

← Back to Sh'elah

Sh'elah is committed to making Torah study accessible to everyone, including people who use assistive technology. This statement explains what we've implemented, what we're still improving, and how to reach us.

1. Our Standard

We design and build Sh'elah against the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA. This is a target we actively design for, not a certification — see Known Limitations below for where we currently fall short.

2. What We've Implemented

  • A 'skip to main content' link on every page for keyboard and screen-reader users
  • Full keyboard operability for menus, the reader settings panel, and modals, with visible focus indicators
  • ARIA roles, labels, and live-region announcements on interactive controls (menus, toggles, the AI answer panel)
  • Color contrast meeting WCAG AA (4.5:1 text, 3:1 UI elements) in both light and dark themes
  • Full right-to-left (RTL) layout mirroring for Hebrew, not just text direction
  • Adjustable text size, font, and line spacing in Reader Settings
  • Respect for your operating system's 'reduce motion' setting — animations are minimized or removed
  • Minimum 44×44px touch targets for interactive elements on touch devices
  • No information is conveyed by color alone — status and state always include text, an icon, or both

3. Known Limitations

In the interest of honesty, not every part of Sh'elah meets this standard yet:

  • Some embedded third-party components (the sign-in widget, calendar picker) follow their own vendor's accessibility implementation, which we don't fully control
  • AI-generated answers are long-form prose and may not always be optimally structured for screen-reader navigation (e.g., heading levels within an answer)
  • Some historical source texts (scanned or OCR'd Hebrew manuscripts) are provided as-is by our source libraries and may have inconsistent markup

We treat each of these as an open item, not a permanent exception, and we prioritize fixes as we find them.

4. Compatibility

Sh'elah is built and tested against current versions of Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge, and is intended to work with modern screen readers such as VoiceOver, NVDA, and JAWS.

5. Feedback & Contact

If you encounter an accessibility barrier anywhere on Sh'elah, please tell us — include the page, what you were trying to do, and the assistive technology you were using if relevant. We will do our best to respond within a reasonable time and fix genuine barriers.
